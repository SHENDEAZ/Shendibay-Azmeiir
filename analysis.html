Analysis

The analysis on the topic "Security in 4G and 5G mobile networks" can be presented as follows:

The analysis will be carried out in three main points, for the sake of infomartivity and easy clarity of the topic.

Security mechanisms

4G and 5G networks use various security mechanisms, including authentication, encryption and access control. Authentication protocols, such as AKA (Authentication and Key Agreement), provide authentication of the subscriber and the device to prevent unauthorized access. To ensure data confidentiality, encryption is used at the radio interface level using the AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) protocol. This protects the transmitted information from unauthorized access and interception. Access control is carried out using various mechanisms, such as network routers, firewalls and intrusion detection systems (IDS). They help prevent unauthorized access to network resources and services.

Possible attacks

Authentication attacks include identity forgery or the use of stolen credentials to gain unauthorized access to the network. Attacks on data encryption can include compromised encryption keys or attacks on encryption protocols used on the network. Access control attacks can be aimed at trying to gain access to resources or services that are not allowed for a specific user.

Feasibility of attacks and their complexity

The feasibility of attacks depends on various factors, such as the level of network protection, the use of strong encryption algorithms and the correct security configuration. Authentication attacks may be feasible in the case of weak or compromised credentials, insufficient authentication, or vulnerabilities in authentication protocols. Data encryption attacks can be difficult if strong encryption algorithms and keys are used, but they can become possible when a vulnerability is discovered.
